Why Save?

Why save money? It is there to spend, is it not? Ultimately, the key is to find a balance of proper spending and saving. The main benefit of saving is that proper savings can give a person more choices in life. A person in a good financial situation has many more feasible options, as opposed to an individual without savings.

Budgeting

The general technique behind developing savings is simple - spend less than you make. There are a variety of ways to accomplish this, but they all come down to the same two variables: income and expenses.

Budgeting is an excellent method to help calculate and visualize these two metrics. There are a multitude of great tools available for these purposes: paper and pencil, Microsoft’s Excel, Google Sheets, and even more sophisticated tools like Intuit’s Mint.

The basic formula comes down to just adding up all sources of income (jobs, side ventures, etc.) and subtracting expenses (rent, auto payments, groceries, etc.) for a set time period, say a month. The key here is realizing where your money is going. Listing everything out can help bring realizations. For example, maybe a large portion of one’s income is going towards eating out or entertainment. This could be seen as an area of improvement to help balance one’s budget - or further increase the amount of money they save monthly.

If your ratio of income to expenses is equal, you have a balanced budget - technically. If you’re left with some extra money at the end of the month, you’re saving. Optimally, a balanced budget includes a portion allocated just for saving. The second key part of saving is setting that money aside, perhaps in a bank, investment account, etc. There are sections on this site that explain these in more detail.

Emergency Funds

An emergency fund is the crux of financial independence. An emergency fund is aptly named - as it is for emergencies. Let’s say your car breaks down - it costs $2,500 to fix and you need it to get to work everyday. That’s something that an emergency fund would be setup to help you get through. Optimally, an emergency fund should be 3-6 months worth of expenses. This allows one some time to sustain themselves from losing a job, health issues, or other crises. An emergency fund should almost always be in cash, as liquidity is important. You need to be able to get those funds now, if a situation would occur.
