Resources

Overview

We've created this site as a beginner's platform to achieve financial wellness; a reader of this blog should leave with a solid base in a multitude of financial topics - and be able to make smart decision regarding their financial health.

As the financial world is incredibly complex, some topics are too complicated or lengthy for this site. However, there are a bunch of additional information and tools out on the web that can supplement this blog.

Here's some of our favorites - please note that each of these sites or tools may offer certain products or services. We aren't sponsored by them, nor do we officially endorse their offerings. Needless to say, from our experience, some of them are worth looking into!

Recommended Resources

Mint

The company that brings you TurboTax also offers a fairly complete portal to link all your finances together. While the service is free, Inuiti analyzes your data, as well as runs ads for certain sponsored products. The advantage is the ability to link all of your credit cards, bank accounts, investments, etc. together to see wholistically where you are at financially.

Investopedia

Investopedia provides its viewers with a huge collection of definitions, explanations, and additional resources for everything from investing strategy to cryptocurrency - think of this site as the Wikipedia for finances.

Khan Academy

You may be familiar with this site - they have a huge collection of quality math and science videos that have gotten many people through college! However, they also have excellent videos on taxes, economics, and much more.

MagnifyMoney - Credit Card Comparison

I can't speak for the rest of the site, but this page is very useful for maximizing your credit card rewards by spending category. The authors seem to keep it up to date, as product offerings change over time.

RetailMeNot

When shopping online, give this site a quick check; they crowdsource promotion codes for a huge collection of sites; nothing feels better than finding a code that gives you an additional discount!

Kinja Deals

Is this list really complete without a shopping site? This site literally has a team of people dedicated to finding deals and promotions online. By warned, they do partner with several companies, meaning that they receive a cut of the sale if you use their link to buy a product. However, they have historically posted some solid deals on electronics, toiletries, apparel, and much more - just be careful not to overshop!
